ShowTheBill

What should a medical procedure actually cost? Built from public data.

How do we know these numbers? · Methodology & limitations · Sources
  • These are estimates from public data, not audited figures.
  • Coverage spans 66 codes in two tiers — 64 of the 70 CMS-specified shoppable services, with 6 documented exclusions (one deleted CPT and five inpatient MS-DRG bundles). 15 Tier-1 imaging codes (MRI, CT, ultrasound, X-ray, mammography) receive a full cost decomposition. 51 Tier-2 codes (14 clinical-lab + 13 E&M + 12 procedure + 12 surgery) show posted prices versus the Medicare published reference only. The procedure picker is grouped by category (Imaging by modality, then Lab, E&M, Procedure, Surgery).
  • Each Tier-1 imaging procedure receives three independent estimates: a bottom-up cost build (labor + equipment + overhead from named assumptions), a CCR cross-check (gross charge × department CCR from each hospital's Medicare cost report), and a Medicare technical-payment reference.
  • CCRs (cost-to-charge ratios) are department-level, not procedure-specific: a blunt instrument. The diagnostic-radiology CCR (HCRIS Worksheet C Part I, line 54) applies to all 15 imaging codes — CT, ultrasound, X-ray, and mammography all share this ratio with MRI.
  • Cost-report data lags ~1–2 years.
  • MRF charges are facility/technical only; the radiologist read fee is billed separately and is excluded throughout.
  • Negotiated rates are payer-specific; we summarize a range (min/median/max).
  • Critical Access Hospitals (CAHs) are reimbursed on a cost basis, not OPPS — handled and labeled separately for Tier-1 codes. For Tier-2 codes at a CAH, the displayed OPPS or other Medicare figure is a national/locality benchmark; CAH outpatient services are actually reimbursed ~101% of cost, but the cost-based override requires a bottom-up cost model that Tier-2 codes don't carry.
  • Medicare reference rates vary by code type. Tier-1 imaging: OPPS technical-only for 12 codes (CY2026, locality-adjusted; cost-based for CAHs); MPFS TC national amount for the 3 mammography codes (status A — not OPPS, not locality-adjusted). Tier-2 E&M/procedure/surgery: OPPS APC rate for most codes (locality-adjusted); for the 5 office-visit CPTs that have no OPPS rate of their own (SI=B/E1), the reference is the OPPS facility clinic-visit fee, HCPCS G0463 (APC 5012, $136.02, locality-adjusted) — the rate billed when a hospital provides a clinic visit in its outpatient department. Therapy 97110 is paid under the MPFS therapy schedule ($29.06 per 15-min unit, national rate, not locality-adjusted). Six codes carry a null reference (one packaged code, three global OB bundles, two preventive visits with no Medicare facility equivalent). Global ECG 93000 (not separately facility-billable) is compared via its 93005 technical-component rate ($60.27) — technical-to-technical match. Tier-2 lab: flat CY2026 CLFS national rate, no wage adjustment. At a CAH, lab and all other Tier-2 references stay at the flat published rate.
  • Tier 1 vs Tier 2. Tier-1 imaging codes get three independent estimates and the convergence chart. Tier-2 codes show posted prices versus Medicare's published rate only — explicitly not a cost estimate. We add a clearly-labeled, narrower comparison rather than imply a decomposed cost we don't have.
  • Per-procedure technologist minutes and equipment capital costs for CT, ultrasound, X-ray, and mammography are labeled low-confidence assumptions (per-site scan volume for non-MRI modalities is not publicly reported). The MRI bottom-up model carries the same caveat.
  • Some rural hospitals legitimately lack certain services — where no price appears in a hospital’s machine-readable file for a given code, we show no price rather than imputing one.
  • The bill checker compares your amount against published prices and estimates in your browser; nothing you enter is transmitted or stored.
  • Add-on intelligence. When a charge runs far above a procedure’s posted price, the bill checker names the most common reason from a curated map (addons.yaml) — e.g. color/duplex Doppler (CPT 93976/93975) billed alongside an ultrasound — and prices that add-on at the same hospital. Every add-on code carries a sourced CY2026 OPPS rate (verbatim Addendum B). We say “commonly”/“often,” never that your specific bill contains it; a code billed instead of the base (a pricier with-contrast scan) is shown as a variant, not flagged as an add-on.
  • Dispute letter. From the itemized check you can generate an editable letter, pre-filled with the hospital, each code and its posted/Medicare price, the quoted-vs-charged gap, and the standard requests (itemized bill, self-pay rate, charity-care policy, Good Faith Estimate / No Surprises Act). It is assembled and downloaded entirely in your browser; for a code we have no benchmark for, it asks for an explanation rather than citing a price. A template, not legal advice.
  • Not medical or financial advice.

Every displayed number traces to a public source or a named assumption.
