Methodology & limitations

  • These are estimates from public data, not audited figures.
  • Coverage spans 29 codes in two tiers: 15 imaging codes (MRI, CT, ultrasound, X-ray, mammography) with a full cost decomposition, and 14 clinical-lab codes shown as posted prices versus Medicare only. The procedure picker is grouped by category (Imaging by modality, then Lab).
  • Each procedure receives three independent estimates: a bottom-up cost build (labor + equipment + overhead from named assumptions), a CCR cross-check (gross charge × department CCR from each hospital's Medicare cost report), and a Medicare technical-payment reference.
  • CCRs (cost-to-charge ratios) are department-level, not procedure-specific: a blunt instrument. The diagnostic-radiology CCR (HCRIS Worksheet C Part I, line 54) applies to all 15 imaging codes — CT, ultrasound, X-ray, and mammography all share this ratio with MRI.
  • Cost-report data lags ~1–2 years.
  • MRF charges are facility/technical only; the radiologist read fee is billed separately and is excluded throughout.
  • Negotiated rates are payer-specific; we summarize a range (min/median/max).
  • Critical Access Hospitals are reimbursed on a cost basis, not OPPS; handled and labeled separately.
  • Medicare reference rates are OPPS technical-only for most codes (CY2026, locality-adjusted). The three mammography codes (77065, 77066, 77067) are OPPS status A — Medicare does not pay them under OPPS — so their reference is the Medicare Physician Fee Schedule technical-component national amount; this rate is not locality-adjusted.
  • Tier 1 vs Tier 2. Tier-1 imaging codes get three independent estimates (bottom-up cost, CCR cross-check, Medicare reference) and the convergence chart. Tier-2 lab codes show posted prices versus Medicare's published rate only — explicitly not a cost estimate. We add a clearly-labeled, narrower comparison rather than imply a decomposed cost we don't have.
  • Lab codes are referenced to the Medicare Clinical Laboratory Fee Schedule (CLFS), CY2026: a single national rate — no wage adjustment and no technical/professional split. At a Critical Access Hospital the lab reference stays the flat national CLFS rate (the cost-based override applies only to the cost-decomposed imaging codes).
  • Per-procedure technologist minutes and equipment capital costs for CT, ultrasound, X-ray, and mammography are labeled low-confidence assumptions (per-site scan volume for non-MRI modalities is not publicly reported). The MRI bottom-up model carries the same caveat.
  • Some rural hospitals legitimately lack certain services — where no price appears in a hospital’s machine-readable file for a given code, we show no price rather than imputing one.
  • The bill checker compares your amount against published prices and estimates in your browser; nothing you enter is transmitted or stored.
  • Not medical or financial advice.
